Brent Dawe
PGSS Representative

My position on the 2015-2016 GSAN committee is PGSS representative. That means I attend monthly meetings of the PGSS council, where I debate/vote for policies & positions that affect graduate students and postdocs at McGill. I also report back to the GSAN committee about PGSS activities and business at council. If you have questions, or if there is as issue you would like raised at council, email me. Thank you for reading about PGSS and GSAN!

Kathleen Xu
VP Finance and Operations

I am VP Finance and Operations for GSAN 2015-2016. Beyond spending most of my time at Douglas Institute trying to decipher the role of dopamine in cognition, you might find me walking around town exploring the culturally vibrant food-scape of Montreal or even reading in a park (if the snow isn't threatening to bury me alive). My experience as GSAN Academic Officer in the past year gave me an understanding of how GSAN functions from the inside, which will aid my work immensely. Meeting the GSAN team this year has convinced me that this is a great opportunity to build a community out of our diverse population, and I hope to continue working on that goal through this new role by meticulous management of our existing resources, while also expanding the search for additional financial support to better serve our community.


Laura-Joy Boulos
President

My name is Laura-Joy Boulos but you can call me Prezie. My major goal as GSAN president matches that of our association: to provide a platform for academic and social interactions between students across all neuroscience research institutes at McGill. I am focusing primarily on strengthening the connections among GSAN members (graduate and post-doctoral). Beyond our beloved McGill, I am also advocating connections with other neuroscience departments in Montreal and in Quebec. In order to do so, our committee is currently working on the first inter-university neuroscience symposium together with UQAM, UdeM and Concordia (enthousiasme quand tu nous tiens!). Finally, I aim to prioritize the use of social media to promote and advertise GSAN activities (photos, website, blog and what not) in order for GSAN to gain visibility. Altogether, I want to participate in the creation of a solid, friendly, gender-equal, neuro-network in which members that fire together wire together. So yea, can’t wait to make this world a better place with each and every one of you.


Meghan Shaw
Douglas Institute Representative

Hi everyone! I am one of your Douglas Mental Health University Institute representatives. As a member of the McGill Group for Suicide Studies, I am currently pursuing a M.Sc. and working on assessing white matter integrity in the uncinate fasciculus as a distinguishing marker between victims of suicide who have experienced child abuse, and those who have not experienced child abuse. My aim is to conduct the first postmortem comparative analysis of myelinated axons and cells in depressed suicides and matched sudden death controls.
